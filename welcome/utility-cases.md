# Utility

### The industry of data is not doing well.&#x20;

Raw audio data is essential for many companies, startups and universities across the world. But the industry of data is not doing well.  Here you can read [this research](https://www.dataprovenance.org/Multimodal_Data_Provenance.pdf) about the current condition of the industry.&#x20;

You will see that there is a significant lack of data presented in audio format, especially from such areas as Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia. Moreover, the majority of audio data comes from audiobooks, TV and radio programs, parliamentary speeches, and podcasts. While it is live, it is often not very emotional and rarely spontaneous. AudataDAO is built to close this gap with live speech audio coming rom messangers.

### Raw audio data is essential for many companies, startups and universities across the world.

#### <mark style="color:green;">Speech Recognition</mark>

Developers training systems like Siri and Alexa need different audio samples to improve accuracy with various accents, languages, and background noises.

#### <mark style="color:red;">Healthcare</mark>

Researchers use audio to create tools that can identify diseases like Parkinson's.

#### <mark style="color:purple;">Entertainment</mark>

Game developers, filmmakers, and other content creators use audio to generate realistic voice acting, create synthetic voices for characters.

#### <mark style="color:orange;">Security</mark>

Security firms apply voice biometrics for identity authentication.

#### <mark style="color:yellow;">Emotion Detection</mark>

Apps for customer service or mental health use tone and pitch to understand feelings.

#### <mark style="color:blue;">Linguistics</mark>

Universities and research centers use audio to study the diversity of languages and oral speech, exploring its peculiarities based on factors such as age, region, occupation, and education.
